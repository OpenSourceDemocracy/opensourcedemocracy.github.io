Vision/Mission


Today we live in a world hanging on to the seat of its pants as we hurdle faster and faster through the most intense period of technological change experienced since the Industrial revolution and soon to be ever.

As a result our current social and political systems are breaking down. Trust in our political leaders, the election process, and the media has hit an all-time low. Local governments have struggled to deliver the services most needed by their communities. Civic engagement and voter turn out numbers have slid lower and lower.

Until now.

Now is the time we come together to bring our democracy into the 21st century.

Now with the ever decreasing costs of computing, the rise of decentralized applications, and the growing mass adoption of information technology, we can build tools to restore faith in our democracy, streamline our local government services, and mobilize informed groups of voters to make their voices heard.

This is our mission: To build a community of dreamers and doers, working together to build the skills and tools needed for an Open Source Democracy.


Why


We are huge fans of open source technologies and the communities built around them. We believe that a true democracy is open sourced in that:

  1. The public has a right to access the source code of government tools and to non-sensitive data
  2. Government and community tools will be best developed by the people who use them

No one feels the negative effects of fake news, poor public transportation, and partisianally drawn district lines more than the people living in our communities. By giving us all an outlet, we can fix these problems.
